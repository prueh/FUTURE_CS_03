Spotting the Hook (Phishing)

Sender Analysis

Verify the actual email domain. Genuine corporate mail never comes from public domains (@gmail, @yahoo).

Artificial Urgency

Attackers use fear. Be wary of "Immediate Action Required" or "Account Expiring in 1 Hour."

Hover Check

Always hover over links to see the real destination URL in the bottom corner of your browser.

The Power of MFA

99%

Attack Prevention

Multi-Factor Authentication is the single most effective barrier against password theft.

Safety Protocols:

  • Use Authenticator Apps (e.g., Google/Microsoft).
  • Never approve a prompt you didn't request.
  • Treat MFA codes like your private password.

Digital Hygiene

Passphrases

Length is strength. Use four random words instead of complex short passwords.

System Updates

Patches fix vulnerabilities. Always restart your machine for security updates.

Lock Screens

Protect your physical workspace. Use "Win + L" whenever you leave your desk.

When in Doubt: REPORT

Suspect a breach or clicked a bad link?

1. Disconnect from Wi-Fi/Network

2. Do NOT enter further credentials

3. Contact the SOC/IT Team Immediately
